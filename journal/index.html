Researching Form Design

13th October 2024

This week, I read an article called Best practices for form design by Salim Ansari. I thought the article was concise and insightful. It clarified the basic principles of form design for me and why they work. There were a lot of things in the article, for example “ask easy questions first,” which seemed pretty intuitive but it was nice to have it laid out and explained regardless. The images that the author chose were also effective. For example, the one chosen for the “provide matching keyboard” tip helped to clarify what the author meant. Overall, the article helped me realize just how much work goes into form design, from layout to design to code-automated features. I look forward to putting these ideas into practice and learning how to create the features discussed in the article.

I don’t encounter form designs too often, but one that I thought was nice is the Stardew Valley character customization form. There is a portrait on the top-left which provides visual reference for the information players are putting in. Everything is on one screen. The labels of required input fields are tinted red when the corresponding input field is blank. Default values are provided for non-essential fields. I like this form because it puts the user input into context in a concise and readable way; it allows interested players to spend time polishing the details, and others to complete it with minimal hassle.
