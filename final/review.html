Expert Project Review

Context

I conducted usability tests with 4 participants from my target demographic. The sampling method was purposive opportunistic sampling. I attended a Playtesting Night held by the Game Development & Arts Club (GDAC) on December 3rd, 2024, and conducted blind playtests there.

  • Participant 1: Cyman Deaver
    • Male
    • Regularly plays games
  • Participant 2: Simon Yoo & Marisol Costales-Juarez
    • Male, Female
    • Casually play games; love mystery games
  • Participant 3: Jasper Fadden O' Roarke
    • Male
    • Game developer; loves RPGs

Task

Participants were asked to solve the first case of my murder mystery game. They were not given additional instruction or assistance.

Key Findings

Below are the five most important observations from my playtest:

  • The game was more challenging than expected: it took playtesters 15 minutes on average, which I permitted.
  • Clicking out of the zoomed-in image also gets rid of the first overlay. Having an 'X' button would be a convenient way for players to return to that state.
  • Most playtesters benefitted from small hints, after which they were able to solve the puzzle. Consider a hint system.
  • Some of the written text is hard to read even zoomed-in. Consider creating an 'eye' icon that shows a typed transcription.
  • Some clues could be made clearer. Participants found it hard to connect a chemical flask symbol to the lab. Jorge's reason for leaving the door open should be hinted.

Changes

I will address finding #2 and #5 for my final submission. The other three are more substantial changes better suited for an ambitious revision.
